About Word of Deliverance Christian Center

Our ministry is a balanced ministry.  Webelieve in strong biblical teaching for the maturation of the saints. Webelieve that the Word of God should be the final authority in the life ofbelievers. The teaching ministries at W.O.D.C.C. focus on teaching theHoly Scripture in an understandable way.Through study and comprehension of the word,we can build a solid foundation of faith to stand on.

A Ministry of Deliverance

We also believe in the gifts of the Spirit. For too long the Body of Christ has ignored the power of the Holy Spiritto bring lasting transformation and power to those who believe.We operate in the gifts of the Spirit andencourage every believer to stir up the gifts that God has given them.                                                                                                                                          

WODCC's History

Word of Deliverance Christian Center was founded in 2008 by Jesus Christ through obedient disciples, Pastor Jason D. King and Lady Rhonda L King.  God was dealing with Pastor King for a long time about starting a church for Him.  God gave him a name for the church in Sept 2007 and Pastor King continued to seek the face of God concerning the timing of this grea twork. The Kings started the ministry with their family and one other committed family. The first Service was held Oct 19, 2008 at The Hampton Innin Summerville, SC. Over 40 people were in attendanceoffering their support and prayers. Our church grew on the word of God and the word of God alone. God then directed us to the Alston Middle School on January 18, 2009. Our praise and worship, choir, Sunday school, ushers, men’s,women’s, hospitality, and pastoral aid ministries were birth here. God blessed us to establish a great foundation. In December of 2009 we moved to a 5000 square foot location on Franchise St in North Charleston. Wewere blessed to add more ministries and have an increase in Co-Laborers to do the work of The Kingdom. Late in 2012 God gave our pastor a vision to clear the church of debt. We partnered with a wonderful church family (Greater Mt Zion Holiness Church) to align ourselves with this vision. In May of 2015 WODCC moved back to Summerville where we began with no debt! The new facility is more functional than the one left in 2012. What God Ordains He Sustains!

Statement of Faith

The Bible

We believe that the Bible is the inspired Word of God and without error in its original manuscript, absolutely infallible, andour source of supreme revelation from God, superior to conscience and reason,though not contrary to reason; and it is therefore our infallible rule of faith and practice.  II Timothy 3:16-17, I Peter 1:23-25, Hebrews 4:12

God

We believe in one God who has revealed Himself in three persons, the Father, the Son and the HolySpirit. The Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit are all co-eternal and all stand equally superior to time. The Eternal FATHER -Deut. 33:27, Psalms 90:2, Psalms 102:27, I Timothy 1:17.  The Eternal SON- John. 1:1-2, John. 8:58, Heb. 1:8, I John 1:2, Micah 5:2, Rev. 1:8 The Eternal SPIRIT -  Heb. 9:14

Jesus The Christ

We believe in the pre-existence, incarnation and virgin birth of Jesus Christ who came into the world to reveal the Father, and was the brightness of His glory and the express image of His person; that Jesus Christ was the Creatorof all things, for by Him the worlds were made.  We further believe that in Christ dwelt all the fullnessof the Godhead bodily and that He was very God and very man. (John. 1:1-2 & 14; I Tim. 3:16, Acts 7:37-38)

Satan and Hell

We believe in the reality and personality of Satan, Job 1:7; Matthew 4:1-11 and that he was defeated by Christ through His death, burial and resurrection at Calvary.  We believe that Hell is a place within the lower parts of the earth reserved for all unrepentant and unbelieving souls.  Rev 20:10-14, Psalm 86:13, Isaiah5:14, Luke16:22-24, Matt 23:33

The Creation of Man

We believe that man was created by a direct and immediate act of God. Genesis 1:26-27

The Fall of Man

We believe that man by transgression fell from a state of righteousnessand holiness in which he was first created, into total spiritual depravity, a state of death in trespasses and sins in which he is held as a slave of sin and an enemy of God, being unable to attain divine righteousness by his ownefforts, but must be redeemed and delivered by the powerof the gospel. Rom 5:12-21; I Cor. 15:1-4

Marriage

We believe that marriage is a committed covenant ordained by God between one man and one woman. Genesis 2:21-25

Repentance

We believe that repentance is a gift from God.  Repentance is dependentupon the convicting action of the Holy Spiritin the lives of sinners and believers.  The convictionof the Holy Spirit, which often accompanies the preachingof the Gospel of Jesus Christ, will result in the revelationof the sinfulness of self which should lead to Godly sorrow. Repentance changes the way we feel and act about sin, self andGod. Repentance is a total change of heart, mind, attitude, emotions,will, action and lifestyle flowing from conviction by the Holy Spiritof sin.  Psalms 51; Matt. 9:12-13.  Acts 17:30

Faith

We believe that faith without works is dead.  We do not believe, asa matter of the fundamental beliefs and doctrines of this church thatany matter can be believed without there being an accompanying and corresponding action.  Our faith is an active faith that requires the doing of acts in the world.  Our faith is not a passive or complacent system of intellectual belief or assent to doctrinebut is an active force in our lives that requires and results in actions being taken. A living, active faith will be manifested not only in belief and mental assent but in actions expressing and based upon that belief.  If a belief is truly held it will be acted upon.  The act is as much a fundamental aspect of our faith as the belief upon which the act is based.  James 2:14-26, Eph 2:8-9 Matt 7:21, Heb1-6

Justification

We believe that repentance and faith toward our Lord Jesus Christ produces the work of justification in the believer. Through faith in the shed blood of Jesus Christ, he is justified and made a partaker of new life in Christ. This is the initial step of salvation. Rom. 5:1-2

Water Baptism

We believe that water baptism is an essential and necessary ordinance of the church performed only upon repentant believers in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit in Jesus’s Name. (Matt 28:19-20)  This act is to be done by immersion and we further believe that it is the means whereby we receive the new covenant sign of "circumcision of heart". Romans 6: 1-11

Baptism in the Holy Spirit

We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit according to Acts 2:4; 10:44-46; 19:6. We also hold that the evidenceof the baptism (or in filling) of the Holy Spirit is one' s response to the Word of God, a Christ-like life, showing forth Christ's character, nature and experiencing and manifesting the fruit of the Holy Spirit.  John 16:14, Gal. 5:22-23, Eph. 5:18-21

Healing

We believe in divine healing for the whole man.  Healing was obtained through the atonement in Christ's sacrificial death.Isaiah 53:4-5. We believe in healing as practiced by the earlychurch. Acts 19:11-12, James 5:14

Grace and Sanctification

We believe that "by grace are ye saved through faith and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not by works lest any man should boast."  (Ephesians 2:8-9) We further believe that the emphasis for a continuous walk in grace should be a heart of righteousness and purity, believing in the keeping power of God, walking after the Spirit and not after the flesh in a lifestyle that demonstrates the character, standards and convictions of Jesus Christ, not being conformed to the world Jude 24, 12:1-2.

Faith

We believe in the five-fold ministries given to the Church at the ascension of Jesus Christ and that they continue to be needed and should be expected today.  We believe in the New Testament order for the local church in its government, structure and worship.  Five-fold (Ascension Gift) Ministries: Apostles, Prophets, Evangelists, Pastors and Teachers. Elders(Titus 1:5-9; I Timothy 3:1-7) Pastoral accountability and authority (Hebrews13:17). Ephesians 4:11-12.  We believe they are called and scripturallyprovided by our Lord Jesus Christ.

Laying on of Hands:

We believei n the doctrine of the laying on of hands for:
1. The confirming ofbelievers in the faith.
2. The confirmation ofone's call by the laying on of hands with prophecy Acts 13:1,3.
3. The impartation of the spiritual gifts when accompanied by prophecy and the laying on of hands.  I Timothy 4:14;  II Timothy1:6
4. The recognition, ordination, and setting apart of ministry.
5. The impartation of the gift of the Holy Spirit Acts 8:17.
6. The ministry of healing the sick.  James 5:14
7. The blessing and dedication (setting apart) of children.  Mark 10:16.
